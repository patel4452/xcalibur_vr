Debunking
the Top 5 Myths
about Air Purifiers

Why having an air purifier can benefit
your well-being .

T

The pandemic, along with such disturbing levels of air pollution have made air purifiers a necessity. Although there is a wealth of information available, air purifiers are not as popular in our country. People still have several misconceptions that raises the question: Are air purifiers worth purchasing? Here are some of the popular beliefs about air purifiers that just aren’t true:

1. All air purifiers work more or less the same.

All air purifiers are different, varying in size and type of filtration technology. Most filters on the market today use High Efficiency Particulate Air (HEPA) technology, whilst others may use carbon technology or even green technology to create active oxygen. Besides that, air purifiers also cater to the different needs of users.

You have basic air purifiers that focus on cleaning the air, as well as more advanced air purifiers that focus on purification points such as to eliminate smoke and odour, help alleviate allergies and asthma, filter viruses and bacteria and even those designed specifically for children and babies.

Choosing the best air purifier for your needs will highly depend on you and your family’s needs. If you or a loved one in your house is a smoker, opting for an air purifier that focuses on eliminating odours and smoke is probably the best choice for you, while someone who has allergies may opt for an air purifier with those focus features. There are also products that feature multiple purification points.

2. They don’t really do anything other than blow air.

When you buy a good quality air purifier, they are typically really quiet when they operate. With barely any sound, its easy to assume that your air purifier is malfunctioning. Contrary to that belief, your air purifiers are actually working hard to keep your indoor air clean.

An air purifier with HEPA technology removes 99.9% or more of all particles which are 0.3 microns in diameter. The reality of it is that it is capable of filtering out particles of any size. When it comes to your home, opting for an air purifier that uses HEPA technology can trap dust, mould, smoke, pet allergens and even bacteria and viruses. These types of quality air purifiers, when used consistently, will help in reducing allergy and asthma symptoms. You’ll especially feel better when you sleep and be less prone to catching illnesses.

Blueair takes its air purification one step further with the HEPASilent technology. The HEPASilent technology catches 99.97% of particles down to 0.1 microns in size and makes a lot less noise while delivering a high flow of clean air.

3. If you have an air-conditioner, you don’t need an air purifier.

An air-conditioner and an air filter are two completely different things. Generally, most air conditioners operate by circulating air in a room and cooling it and has nothing to do with purification of the air or eliminating harmful particles and odours.

Now, you may argue that modern aircons come with inbuilt purification systems, however, what you may not know is that filters in these aircon systems cannot clean the air in a more holistic manner. While they may be able to trap dust particles and pet dander, they cannot eliminate bad odours, mould spores or other harmful substances floating in the air.

4. If there are bad odours in your home, the purifier is not working.

An air-conditioner and an air filter are two completely different things. Generally, most air conditioners operate by circulating air in a room and cooling it and has nothing to do with purification of the air or eliminating harmful particles and odours.

While the level of odour in the room is one way of determining the quality of an air purifier, its not a full-proof method. This would depend on the kind of filter that your air purifier uses. An air purifier with filtration technology such as that in Medklinn’s patented Cerafusion technology create Active Oxygen. Just as nature does, it works to help eliminate pollutants in the air and on surfaces, as well as odours such as the stench of cigarettes, pets, food or even that musky mould smell.

5. You need to be close to the air purifier for it to work.

his isn’t true because air purifiers don’t work that way. In fact, each air purifier comes with its own Clean Air Delivery Rate (CADR), which describes the area size of which the air purifier is most effective. You can be sitting anywhere within that specified area to be able to benefit from the air purifier.

The CADR of an air purifier makes a huge difference. Both Medklinn and Blueair cater for a wide range of spaces, from small spaces such as in a car to industrial office spaces. It is all about finding the right CADR for your space.

However, you should be aware that matching the size to your space is important. You shouldn’t get an air purifier meant to be used in a car for your bedroom because it will be ineffective. Alternatively, too big of an air purifier would more than likely cost you a lot more to maintain in the long run.

Overall, air purifiers have become a vital part of our modern everyday living and when used properly, can greatly improve one’s health.
