Air Purifiers

Why having an air purifier can

benefit your well-being .

D

espite the wealth of information available, many people still have misconceptions about air purifiers. Here are some of the common ones:

 

1. All air purifiers work more or less the same.

A

ll air purifiers are different, varying in size and the type of filtration technology. Most purifiers on the market today use High Efficiency Particulate Air (HEPA) technology, whilst others may use carbon or green technology to create active oxygen.

Basic air purifiers focus primarily on cleaning the air while more advanced air purifiers may focus on selected purification points such as to eliminate smoke and odour, help alleviate allergies and asthma, as well as filter viruses and bacteria. There are also a number of air purifiers that are designed specifically for children and babies.

Choosing the best air purifier will highly depend on you and your family’s needs. If you or a loved one in your house is a smoker, opting for an air purifier that focuses on eliminating odours and smoke is probably the best choice for you, while someone who has allergies may opt for an air purifier with those focus features.

 

2. Air purifiers don’t really do anything other than blow air

A

n air purifier with HEPA technology removes 99.9% of harmful particles which can be as small as 0.3 microns in diameter. Because this special technology can trap dust, mould, smoke, pet allergens, bacteria and viruses, the latest air purifiers allow for better sleep and ensure you and your family are less prone to catching illnesses.

Blueair takes its air purification one step further with the HEPASilent technology. The HEPASilent technology catches 99.97% of particles down to 0.1 microns in size and makes a lot less noise while delivering a high flow of clean air.

 

3. If you have an air-conditioner, you don’t need an air purifier.

A

n air-conditioner and an air purifier are two completely different things. Generally, most air conditioners operate by circulating air in a room and cooling it in the process. These machines are not designed to eliminate harmful particles or remove odours.

You may argue that modern aircons come with inbuilt purification systems. In most instances however, even the more costly air conditioners are only able to trap dust particles and pet dander.

 
“Making the right choice in air purifiers can lead to not just healthier lives but more cash in your wallet.”
 

4. If there are bad odours in your home, the purifier is not working.

W

hile breathing in foul odour in a room is one way of determining the quality of an air purifier, it is not a fool proof method.

An air purifier with filtration technology such as that in Medklinn’s patented Cerafusion technology creates Active Oxygen. It works to help eliminate pollutants in the air and on surfaces, as well as trapping odours such as the stench of cigarettes, pets, food and mould.

 

5. You need to be close to the air purifier for it to work.

E

ach air purifier comes with its own Clean Air Delivery Rate (CADR), which describes the area size of which the air purifier is most effective. You can be sitting anywhere within that specified area benefit from the air purifier.

Both Medklinn and Blueair carry air purifiers that suit a wide range of spaces, such as inside a car to industrial office spaces. It is all about finding the right CADR for your needs.

While it is obvious that a small air purifier will be ineffective in large spaces, too big of an air purifier would cost you a lot more to maintain in the long run.
