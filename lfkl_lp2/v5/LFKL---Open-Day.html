A FRENCH SCHOOL IN A MULTILINGUAL ENVIRONMENT

 ​French Language Bridging Program

The LFKL offers a French bridging program and an early English program designed to welcome international students in primary school students. Students participate in an immersion class 10 hours a week given by a teacher specialised in teaching French as a foreign language.


Learn more

A Worldwide Network Of French schools

The LFKL is part of the AEFE (Agency for French Education Abroad), a global network of 522 French schools in 139 countries that welcome 370 000 pupils. French schools abroad share a unique curriculum, allowing students to pursue their studies anywhere around the world.The Agency gives Excellence-Major scholarships to foreign bachelors from the network according to their level of excellence in order to pursue their studies in France.

Baccalaureate with an international exposure

French schools abroad share a unique curriculum, allowing students to pursue their studies anywhere around the world. Every year students leave the LFKL to study in prestigious institutions and universities in France and the European Union, United Kingdom, USA, Canada, Australia, Switzerland, Singapore and Middle-East.

ACADEMIC YEARS

EXPAND THE MIND OF YOUR CHILDREN

Preschool

Hevea welcomes children from ages  3 to 8 in a safe, spacious and green environment. Hevea is open to non-French speaking students from the “Très Petite Section” (Pre-nursery) to the “CE2” (year 4 of primary school). The school provides new facilities such as swimming pool, a multipurpose hall, a library, a colourful playground, a canteen, activity and napping rooms adapted to younger children.

Elementary School

From 6 years old to 11 years old, the focus of the elementary school is reading, writing and mathematics. Subjects such as science, geography and history are gradually introduced.  CP is the first class in the Elementary School which goes up to the CM2 class. Normally the students spend five years in elementary school.

Middle School

From 12 years old to 14 years old, various subjects are taught to students. These include French, Mathematics, History and Geography, Civic Education, Life and Earth Sciences, Technology, Art, Musical Education, Physical Education, Physics and Chemistry, two Modem languages as well as the History of Art.

High School

From 15 years old to 18 years old. Once LFKL students enter High school, they start preparing for their final examination, the French Baccalaureate, that enables them to pursue their future education, in renowned French and international universities.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

What is the LFKL enrolment process?

For non-French-speakers, we advise you to request an appointment to learn more about the curriculum before applying. Kindly contact our marketing manager by email to schedule a visit: marketing@lfkl.edu.my

You could then register online under the admissions section: https://www.lfkl.edu.my/admission-lfkl/

What is the student and teacher ratio at the school?

The LFKL currently brings together 600 students from 38 different nationalities. Our classes are offered by 70 qualified teachers attached to the Ministry of National Education and by professional native English teachers.

There are 20 to 23 students per class in kindergarten and in elementary school. In kindergarten, a French teacher, an English teacher (present half of the time) and an assistant supervise each class. In elementary school, the English teacher is present 1/3rd of the time.

Can Malaysian students be enroled in the French school?

For Malaysian students to start school at the LFKL, their school applications must be approved by the MOE first.

The school will be sending the family the MOE form that will have to be filled in Bahasa, once the form has been completed by the family, the school will send it over to the MOE.

Can my child receive a French-language education if he/she does not know how to speak French or English?

Yes, depending on his or her age. Anybody can submit an application to our French school. This request will be reviewed by an admission test after the kindergarten stage. Our aim is to identify your child’s needs.

Special measures are implemented by school to assist both the learner, his or her family to better integrate the community, and make sure students meet with success at school.

When can my child start school? What are the possible enrolments?

Children could start school anytime during the year but it is preferable for students to start school in September.

Primary school students from CP/year 1 (6 years old) not coming from the French educational system that would like to join the school anytime during the year will have to go through an admission test. Secondary and high school students start school in September; however, other intakes are possible during the year if there are still availabilities.
