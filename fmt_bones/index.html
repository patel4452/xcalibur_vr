5 dietary ingredients that help build and maintain healthy bones

October 27, 2022 10:53 AM

Incorporating certain ingredients in a diet can prove effective in building healthier bones.

Once you’ve reached the age of 30, you would have achieved peak bone mass. However, if not enough bone mass is created during this time, you may have an increased risk of developing fragile bones. The lack of required minerals obtained from the food you eat can also lead to many bone-deteriorating diseases such as osteoporosis and rickets.

The problem is that diseases such as osteoporosis show no symptoms, thus going unnoticed until patients experience a fracture.

In a 2021 news report backed by the Fragility Fracture Network Malaysia and Malaysian Osteoporosis Society, more than 8.9 million fractures occur every year globally. Furthermore, among the elderly Malaysians, an estimated 77% of Malaysian women live with post-menopausal osteoporosis.

The majority of patients who have hip fractures then end up disabled, needing daily nursing care or are placed in nursing homes.

Due to the seriousness of the disease and the increasing number of elderly in the country, there is a vital need for the population to start eating healthier and increase bone health.

Glucosamine

Glucosamine is naturally found in shellfish.

Glucosamine is found naturally in the body. It plays an important role in making glycosaminoglycans and glycoproteins, which are essential building blocks of many parts of the joints, such as the ligaments, tendons, cartilage and synovial fluid.

Glycosaminoglycans have a crucial role in the cell signalling process that includes the regulation of cell growth, proliferation, promotion of cell adhesion, anticoagulation as well as wound repair. Meanwhile, glycoproteins act as natural neutralising targets for antibodies generated by the body in fighting off infections.

Natural sources of glucosamine are often found in shellfish including shrimp, lobster and crabs.

Vitamin K

Vitamin K is abundantly found in celery and other dark leafy green vegetables.

Vitamin K has an established biological function in maintaining bone health. Vitamin K promotes the transition of osteoblasts (large cells that are responsible for the synthesis and mineralisation of bone) to osteocytes (bone cells found in mature bone tissue). In simple terms, vitamin K is required for bone formation and sustaining the overall health of bones.

Additionally, the recommended daily intake of vitamin K for men is 120mcg daily and 90mcg for women.

Vitamin K can be found in celery and other dark leafy green vegetables. To put this into context, 100g of celery have approximately 32mcg of vitamin K.

Calcium

Calcium-rich foods such as dairy products aid in building and sustaining denser bones.

It is widely known that calcium works its magic in building and maintaining strong bones. However, many people do not realise the equal importance of Vitamin D. Calcium helps build and maintain bones, while vitamin D helps your body effectively absorb calcium. With that said, even if you’re consuming enough calcium a day, it could be going to waste if you’re deficient in vitamin D.

Some foods that are high in calcium include dairy products such as milk, cheese, and soy drinks.

Vitamin D

Salmon and other fatty fish are rich in Vitamin D and essential omega-3.

As previously stated, Vitamin D helps your body absorb much-needed calcium. Sunlight is the main source of vitamin D, however, staying in the sun without adequate skin protection puts you at risk of skin cancer.

If you’re worried about this risk that comes with sunbathing, foods such as egg yolks, red meat and fatty fish can provide you with your necessary daily intake of vitamin D.

Protein

Animal-based proteins provide essential amino acids our body requires

Protein makes up roughly 50% of the volume of a bone and about one-third of its mass. Therefore, an adequate supply of amino acids is needed to support the formation and maintenance of bone.

While animal-based foods such as meat, poultry, eggs, fish, and dairy are known to be good sources of complete protein, plant-based foods such as nuts, seeds and fruits are also great protein sources for vegans and vegetarians alike.

It is unfortunate, however, that many people do not obtain a sufficient intake of the above nutrients in their daily diet. Poor food choices and busy lifestyles further exacerbate the problem. Hence, another way to help obtain these minerals and nutrients is through dietary supplements. Luckily, there are many in the market that use various ingredients to suit different needs.

Dietary supplements provide adequate amounts of essential nutrients that are lacking in one's diet. Popular examples of dietary supplements that are beneficial for bone health include multivitamins, fish oil, calcium tablets and even ginseng root. While these supplements are not to take the place of healthy eating habits, they work as a supporting role to your diet.

To receive the best supplement recommendation and dietary plans in order to improve your overall bone health, it is advisable to consult your doctor, dietician or nutritionist.
