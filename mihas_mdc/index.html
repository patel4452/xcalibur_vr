MIHAS 2021 Revitalises
Malaysia’s Halal
Economy Amid
the Pandemic

Adapting to the Pandemic by
Going Virtual

The Malaysia International Halal Showcase (MIHAS) is an annual trade fair organised by the Malaysia External Trade Development Corporation (MATRADE). Inaugurated in 2004, MIHAS provides global halal suppliers and buyers a credible platform to network and trade. MIHAS has significantly contributed to Malaysia's halal economy in terms of revenue and reputation. This is in line with MATRADE's objective to strengthen the country's position as the world's leading halal hub.

Themed “Empowering Halal, Tomorrow, Together,” MIHAS 2021 was the 17th edition of the showcase. With the pandemic at hand, MIHAS pioneered its first virtual showcase and adapted by incorporating revolutionary technological capabilities. This enabled foreign exhibitors to get involved in the event despite the logistical and security obstacles brought upon by the global pandemic. MIHAS 2021 has also played a vital role in reinforcing Malaysia’s economic recovery phase.

“MIHAS 2021 provides exhibitors with a better

understanding of their target markets and unlocks

opportunities to connect with halal business

partners worldwide.”

MIHAS 2021 generated RM2.13Billion in total revenue, a 33% increase from 2019.

MIHAS 2021 attracted 246 local exhibitors and 303 foreign exhibitors from 42 countries including first-time participants from Mexico, Guatemala, Colombia and Trinidad and Tobago.

The countries with the highest number of exhibitors were Indonesia (45 exhibitors), China (23 exhibitors), Philippines (20 exhibitors), South Korea (18 exhibitors) and Thailand (16 exhibitors.)

Local exhibitors achieved sales amounting to RM562 million. International exhibitors were also equally successful, recording the total sales of RM818 million. The most successful international companies were exhibitors from Indonesia who noted sales worth RM403.4 million, followed by the Philippines (RM 199.3 million), Vietnam (RM103.8 million), Pakistan (RM36.0 million), and Brazil (RM13.2 million.) The revenue secured by both local and international companies further strengthened MIHAS and Malaysia’s reputation as the leading trade gateway to global halal markets.

Among the internationally recognised companies that took part in INSP MIHAS 2021 were Japan’s leading car manufacturer, Mitsubishi Corporation, internationally famed retail franchise, Daiso, and GS Retail.

MIHAS 2021 also saw participation from government agencies and ministries with the involvement of 100 virtual booths; among which includes Majlis Amanah Raya (MARA), Ministry of International Trade and Industry (MITI), Ministry of Agriculture & Food Industries (MAFI), Federal Agricultural Marketing Authority (FAMA), and more.
