Retirement savings help stabilise your financial future in an ever-changing economy

October 12, 2022 10:53 AM

Amidst a post-pandemic economic recovery, the Malaysian workforce grapples with a serious consequence of COVID-19: price inflation that continues to erode our spending power.

According to the Department of Statistics Malaysia, yearly inflation rose from negative 1.2% to as much as 2.5% from 2020 to 2021. Additionally, a consumer price index for June 2022 reported that annual inflation had increased by a further 0.9% with food and beverage and transportation as huge contributors.

In comparison, average wage rates had fallen by 9% at the mean level and a staggering 15.6% at the median level during the year of 2020. The salaries and wage growth for 2022 have not been reported as of this article.

With rising inflation putting a bigger burden on finances, many individuals are choosing to forgo retirement planning and instead focus on immediate concerns, particularly among the younger workforce.

The rise of the gig economy

The rise of social media and the digital economy have created platforms for unique job opportunities.

In a recent news report, nearly 72.1% of SPM graduates are looking to get into the gig economy as freelancers, in the form of social media influencers, e-hailing taxi drivers and food delivery riders.

As there is currently no mandatory savings fund for freelance work that is equivalent to the Employees Provident Fund, this could lead to a significant portion of this younger workforce being unprotected or left behind when it comes to retirement savings.

Building good financial habits and taking the time to properly plan your retirement funds is especially important – and the sooner, the better.

Building good saving habits early

Building up a habit of saving early in your career will keep you ahead of the curve in living a fruitful retirement.

With such grave financial concerns for Malaysian youth, Private Pension Administrator Malaysia’s chief executive officer Husaini Hussin encourages them to begin planning for retirement as soon as they enter the workforce.

“The idea behind retirement planning when you’re young is not so much getting the right amount, it is more about building the habit of always thinking about retirement. There is a saying in Malay that goes, “Sikit-sikit, lama-lama jadi bukit”.

A good start would be to plan according to how much is needed for day-to-day necessities and how much can be set aside for retirement savings, no matter how small.

Husaini also adds that fresh graduates earning their first few paychecks should be vigilant in their savings but not necessarily torment themselves; as their income grows over time, they are always able to revisit their savings proportion for the benefit of their future retirement savings.

Private Retirement Schemes are a great stepping stone towards diversifying your retirement efforts, whether you are a freelancer or a contract worker. It provides a voluntary long-term savings and investment scheme that caters towards your own retirement needs, goals and risk appetite.
