What is MOOC

Massive Open Online Courses (MOOCs) are online courses available for anyone with a computer and access to the internet. MOOCs provide an affordable and flexible way to learn new skills for career advancement and deliver quality educational experiences at scale. MOOCs offer students a way to learn in a setting like an online class but are usually loosely structured and can be accessed without committing to an academic programme (MQA,2019)

• The keyword “massive” is associated with the capacity of the course offered to serve large numbers of learners
• “Open Online” refers to MOOCs that can be accessed anywhere by connecting to the Internet to provide synchronous and/or asynchronous interactions between the participants (instructors and learners) and the content of the course.
• A MOOC is facilitated by at least an instructor whether it is offered as an in-session or a self-paced mode.

Description

This course provides an overview of the social dimension of the engineering profession. The course introduces the professional bodies and code of conduct of the engineer. The need for sustainable development, occupational safety and health, legal and ethical issues in engineering practice and intellectual property rights will be discussed. The common societal and cultural issues that crop up in engineering practice will also be covered.

This courseware is structured to cover 6 main units (chapter):

· The Professional Engineer
· Route to Professional Engineer
· Sustainability Engineering
· Legal Requirement & Professional Ethics
· Intellectual Property (IP)
· Molding the Engineer’s World View

Throughout this courseware, learners will be introduced to the requirements of achieving the Professional Engineer recognition, the required conduct of an engineer in upholding the status of the profession and providing an insight into moulding the engineer’s world view. The courses focuses particularly on responsibilities and expectations towards adaptation to the society as an engineer.

For each unit, specific goals and objectives are set, and learners will be gauged on their achievement of the goals and objectives through quizzes and activities in each related topic. Examples will be given; intended to cause learners to reflect and apply the learned concepts plus strategies to their own situations and context.

This course is intended to provide young engineers with the necessary skills, attitude and mindset to enable them to perform professionally in their future career and to contribute positively to the society.

introduction

Pengajian Malaysia III is a subject specially designed by the Ministry of Higher Education of Malaysia for international students who pursue their studies at the degree level in Malaysia. This subject is a compulsory subject that should be taken at any time throughout their studies at the institution of higher learning before the students graduate.

This course discusses History and Politics, Malaysian Constitution, National Administrative System and Structure, Society and Unity, National Development and Religion and Beliefs. This course aims to cultivate the spirit of universality. Teaching and learning will be implemented in the form of experience-based learning through team activities inside and outside the lecture including lectures, assignments, social experiments, presentations and examinations.

At the end of this course, students are expected to develop self-confidence, self-awareness, ethics and professionalism as well as be able to apply communication skills, leadership and teamwork in managing daily life.

Course Description

Pengajian Malaysia III is a compulsory General Subject (MPU) course for international students. This course is worth 3 credit hours in the Bachelor's Degree program. This course contains 7 units. Each unit is expected to take 14 hours. To complete the entire course, students must use at least 120 hours of study time. There are no prerequisites for this course.

The purpose of this elective course offered is to provide international students with in-depth knowledge of Malaysia. This course emphasizes both theoretical and application aspects to help students understand and analyse various aspects of Malaysia that include learning about the historical aspect, ruling and legislation system, important policies, socioeconomic development of the nation, community, unity, and current issues that affect the nation's development

COURSE objectives

  • To describe and discuss diversity in society 
  • To commenting on the importance of malaysian identity towards cultivating the spirit of universality. 
  • To build social relationships and interactions among students.

Course Outcomes

  • CO1: understand Malaysia’s history and society.
  • CO2: discuss the development of society in the sense of politics, economy and sociocultural.
  • CO3: analyse Malaysia's roles and contributions in the international arena.
  • CO4: appreciate the differences of Malaysian society.

Description

This course is an introductory course in management theory and practice. Management is presented as a discipline and as a process. This course introduces the modern management concepts with emphasis on the major functions and activities performed by a manager. It will discuss the behaviour of individuals and groups in organizational settings. Also included are the historical theories, decision- making processes, interpersonal concepts and current management issues.

This courseware is structured to cover 10 main units (chapter):

· Managing in Today’s World
· Management Environment, Culture and Global Economy
· Ethics and Corporate Social Responsibility
· Decision Making
· Planning in an Organization
· Organizational Structure and Design
· Human Resource Management
· Managing Teams
· Elements of Behaviour in Organizations
· Monitoring and Controlling

Throughout the use of this courseware, learners will be presented with concepts, strategies, developments and guidance on the basic principles of management. These focuses particularly on the management environment, four functions of management, and etc. A comprehensive coverage on the real examples will also be given.

For each unit, specific goals and objectives are provided, along with quizzes and activities whereby learners could use to gauge their level of understanding in each related topic. Examples will be given; intended to cause learners to reflect and apply the learned concepts plus strategies to their own situations and context.

The implementation and use of this course is immensely looked forward to increase learners understanding about the principles of management.

Course objectives

  • To provide the student with a foundation of the concept of management, recognizing it as both an art and a science.
  • To acquaint the student with the management process that includes understanding the theory and practice of management.
  • To provide the student with an understanding of the four major functions of management: planning, organizing, leading, controlling and their application in today’s workplace.
  • To gain an appreciation for the scope and complexity of business decision making and information needs.
  • Be able to organize business information and structure decision problems.

Course outcomes

  • CO1 - Identify the historical development of management from traditional to modern concepts. (C2)
  • CO2 - Explain management from global context and the needs for business to act ethically and being socially responsible towards stakeholders. (C4)
  • CO3 - Describe the concepts and theories in the main functions of management; planning, organizing, leading, and controlling. (A3)
  • CO4 - Analyze basic management tools to the main functions of management in solving organizational problems. (C4)
